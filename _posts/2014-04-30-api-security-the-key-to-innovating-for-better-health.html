Innovation in the healthcare industry seems to face a constant uphill battle.Doctors are relatively conservative, many processes are manual, and patients continually feel like they have to fill out forms, over, and over and over again.Innovations in information technology, which are rapidly available to wider industries, feel like they reach the healthcare at a glacial pace, or worse, never.Major innovations and trends such as cloud computing, the consumerization of IT, mobile enablement and API management are just a few of these examples.Adoption aside, the problem is worsened by the pesky issue of HIPAA compliance as well as data confidentiality, let alone cyber-attacks on medical information.
